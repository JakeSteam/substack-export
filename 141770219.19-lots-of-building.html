From elsewhere on the internet

  • Trivia: This is slightly tech focused, but Google’s “PageRank” is named because it ranks pages, right? MySQL is named because it’s My SQL, right? Nope, they’re named after people like Larry Page and My Widenius"! Main Street, San Francisco? Named after Charles Main, obviously. There’s plenty more baffling examples.

  • TV: BBC’s “The Space Shuttle That Fell to Earth” is an excellent miniseries about Columbia’s reentry disaster primarily told through interviews and archive footage. It’s deeply engaging without any unnecessary dramatics, and is by Louis Theroux (not included) & his Mindhouse company.

  • Taskmaster: I’m a massive fan of Taskmaster, as is most of the UK. However, I somehow missed that there was an official Taskmaster podcast hosted by Ed Gamble, discussing episodes with all of the guests. It’s great being reminded of older seasons, and hearing from old favourites.
